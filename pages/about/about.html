Who we are

The Ergo Lab.

Group 4 — CIP 1101-CSAD. We are eight students who built sensoria to bridge the gap between wearable technologies and human physiology, making the science of smart devices accessible to everyone.

Our Purpose

Built to make wearable science accessible.

The Ergo Lab. created sensoria to explain how smart devices utilize real-time sensors and feedback to actively correct poor posture, reduce muscle strain, and optimize physical movement — so that anyone can understand and use these technologies to build healthier, pain-free habits.

Evidence-Based Content

Every article is grounded in peer-reviewed research and real deployment data — no speculation, no hype.

Written for Everyone

From desk workers to elite athletes to curious technologists — sensoria speaks in plain language without sacrificing depth.

Independent & Ad-Free

No sponsored content, no affiliate links. Our only obligation is to the reader and the truth.

How we got here

The Story Behind sensoria

sensoria began as a class project for CIP 1101-CSAD — Computer Integration in Professional Practice. What started as a wireframe became a full pre-development plan, and what started as a plan became a website with a clear mission: make wearable science readable for everyone.

Phase 1 · January 2026
Concept & Team Formation
Group 4 forms as The Ergo Lab. Topic selected: the intersection of wearable technology and human ergonomics. Role assignments finalized.
Phase 2 · February 2026
Pre-Development Plan
Purpose statement, target audience, sitemap, navigation plan, wireframes, color palettes, font pairings, and folder structure defined and documented.
Phase 3 · March 2026
Content Research & Drafting
Five topic pillars researched. Article drafts written for Biomechanics, Feedback & Behavior, Case Studies, Engineering, and Ethics.
Phase 4 · March–April 2026
Design & Development
Visual identity finalized. HTML/CSS/JS development of homepage, section pages, article templates, and case study templates completed.
Phase 5 · April 2026
Testing, QA & Launch
Cross-browser and mobile testing, accessibility review, content proofreading, and final site submission for CIP 1101-CSAD.
